13 Complete Lunar Ceremonies for Each Moon of the Year

Palis Global · Digital Edition

How to Work with This Deck

Each ritual card corresponds to one of the 13 Full Moons of the year (including the Blue Moon). Print and cut them, or use them digitally. Read each card in the three days surrounding the Full Moon. The rituals range from 10 minutes to one hour — choose the depth that feels right for your current practice.

All you need is intention, quiet, and the materials listed. The ritual works as much with your willingness as it does with the tools.

The 13 Moon Rituals

🌕
Wolf Moon · January
Duration: 20 minutes · Best performed: midnight

The Wolf Moon howls for clarity in the cold. This is the moon of raw truth — what must be released before the new cycle can begin? Light a white candle. Write three things you are carrying from the previous year that no longer serve you. Read them aloud. Burn the paper (safely). Watch the smoke carry them away.

Tarot pairing: Draw one card for what the year ahead needs you to release first.

You'll need: White candle · Paper · Pen · Fireproof bowl
🌕
Snow Moon · February
Duration: 15 minutes · Best performed: before sleep

February's Full Moon falls in the quiet of winter's deepest hold. This is a moon for self-love and inner warmth. Draw a bath or find a comfortable place to sit wrapped in something soft. Place your hands on your heart. Ask: what part of me needs the most tenderness right now? Write the answer. Sleep with it under your pillow.

Tarot pairing: Draw one card for what your heart is asking for.

You'll need: Warm bath or blanket · Journal · Rose quartz (optional)
🌕
Worm Moon · March
Duration: 30 minutes · Best performed: outside if possible

The earth begins to wake. Worm Moon energy supports fresh beginnings and setting the seeds of what you want to grow this year. Write 5 intentions — not goals, but states of being. "I am becoming someone who..." End each sentence. Read them facing the moon.

Tarot pairing: Three-card spread — seed / soil / sun (what you're planting, what supports it, what will help it grow).

You'll need: Journal · Pen · Access to moonlight (window works)
🌕
Pink Moon · April
Duration: 45 minutes · Best performed: at dusk

The Pink Moon (named for blooming phlox) governs love, connection, and the opening of the heart after hardship. This is the most powerful moon for love readings. Perform the Lovers' Mirror spread (from the Oracle Guide) tonight. Burn rose incense if available. Ask: who is it time to love more fully — myself, or another?

Tarot pairing: Full Constellation spread for matters of the heart.

You'll need: Tarot deck · Rose incense or oil · Pink or red candle
🌕
Flower Moon · May
Duration: 25 minutes

Everything blooms now. Flower Moon energy is abundant, fertile, and radiantly alive. This is a moon to celebrate what has grown. Write a gratitude letter — not a list, but an actual letter — to yourself, acknowledging all you have cultivated this year. Read it aloud.

You'll need: Journal · Fresh flowers (optional, any kind)
🌕
Strawberry Moon · June
Duration: 20 minutes

The summer solstice moon. Longest light. This moon asks: where in your life are you still hiding? The Strawberry Moon calls for full visibility. Write down one way you have been making yourself smaller than you are. Then write how you would show up if you stopped.

You'll need: Journal · Gold or yellow candle
