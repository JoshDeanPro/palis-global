Shadow Work

40 Guided Prompts for Descending into Truth
and Returning Whole

Palis Global · Digital Edition

A Letter Before You Begin

"The shadow is not your enemy. It is the part of you that was asked to be silent for so long that it forgot it had a voice. This journal is an invitation to listen."

Shadow work is the practice of turning your attention toward what you normally avoid — the uncomfortable emotions, the patterns you repeat, the parts of yourself you've been taught to hide or deny. It is not dramatic or dangerous. It is, in fact, the most tender act of self-love available.

These 40 prompts move in phases, like the moon: from the New Moon of awareness, through the waxing of excavation, to the Full Moon of revelation, and into the waning integration. Each section is complete in itself. You may work in order or let the prompts call to you.

Write without editing. The shadow speaks in first drafts.

How to Use This Journal

Find a quiet space. Light a candle if you have one. Read the prompt. Wait 60 seconds before writing — let it settle into your body. Then write without stopping for at least 10 minutes. Do not reread until the next day.

Some prompts will feel easy. Others will create resistance. The resistance is always pointing toward something worth examining.

Phase I · New Moon

Awareness · The beginning of seeing

Prompt 01
What emotion do you most often push away when it arises? What do you tell yourself to justify the pushing?
Prompt 02
What do you judge most harshly in other people? Write it down plainly, without softening it. Then ask: where does this quality live in me?
Prompt 03
What are you most afraid others will discover about you? Write it here, for no audience but yourself.
Prompt 04
Describe the version of yourself you perform for the world. Now describe the version that only emerges when you are completely alone.
Prompt 05
What story about yourself do you repeat most often? What would happen if that story were simply... not true?
🌒

Phase II · Waxing Crescent

Excavation · Going deeper

Prompt 11
What childhood wound still shapes your behavior in adult relationships? Be specific — name the wound, not just the feeling.
Prompt 12
When do you feel most unlovable? What specific thought pattern arises in those moments? Who first made you feel this way?
Prompt 16
Write a letter to the version of you that is most afraid. Not to comfort them — just to acknowledge them. Tell them you see them.
🌕

Phase III · Full Moon

Revelation · The full light

Prompt 21
What truth about yourself have you known for a long time but refused to fully acknowledge? Write it plainly, in one sentence first. Then expand.
Prompt 25
Describe a quality in yourself that you have always seen as a flaw. Now reframe it: in what context is this quality actually a gift?
🌘

Phase IV · Waning Crescent

Integration · Returning with what you found

Prompt 36
What part of your shadow have you begun to accept through this work? Write it a small welcome. You don't have to celebrate it — just stop fighting it.
Prompt 40
Write a final letter — from your integrated self to your shadow self. Not to dismiss it. To invite it fully into the light, as part of who you are.
